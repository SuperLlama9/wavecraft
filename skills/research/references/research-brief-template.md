# Research Brief Template

Write the research brief to `docs/research/RESEARCH-BRIEF.md` or `docs/research/RESEARCH-BRIEF-[topic].md`.

```markdown
# Research Brief: [Topic]

## Date: [date]
## Researcher: [name] + Claude

## Problem Statement
[2-3 sentences: what problem are we solving? For whom? Why does it matter?]

## User / Stakeholder Needs

### [Persona 1: Role Name]
- Current workflow: [how they solve this today]
- Pain points: [what's frustrating]
- Jobs to be done: [what they need to accomplish]
- Success criteria: [how they'd measure "this works"]

### [Persona 2: Role Name]
...

## Competitor / Existing Solutions

### [Solution 1]
- URL: [link]
- Strengths: [specific features, UX patterns]
- Weaknesses: [what's missing]
- Pricing: [model and range]
- Key takeaway: [what we can learn]

### [Solution 2]
...

## Technical Feasibility
- Stack compatibility: [can we build this with our current stack?]
- Third-party dependencies: [APIs, services needed]
- Data requirements: [what data, where from, privacy implications]
- Performance considerations: [expected load, response time needs]
- Technical risks: [risk → likelihood → impact → mitigation]

## Market Context
[If applicable:]
- Market size: [TAM/SAM/SOM or qualitative assessment]
- Pricing benchmarks: [what competitors charge]
- Regulatory: [compliance requirements]
- Timing: [market trends, competitive windows]
[Or: "N/A — internal feature / process automation"]

## Key Findings
1. [Most important insight from the research]
2. [Second most important insight]
3. [Third most important insight]

## Open Questions
- [Question 1 — needs further investigation or user input]
- [Question 2]

## Recommendation
[Proceed / Pivot / Park — with rationale based on the findings above]
```

## Tips

- Keep the brief focused — 1-2 pages is ideal
- Findings should be actionable, not just informational
- The recommendation should be clear and defensible
- Open questions should specify what's needed to answer them
